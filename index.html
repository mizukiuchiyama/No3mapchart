Obesity Rates Surge in 23 States, Highlighting Need for Community-Driven Solutions

Experts call for investments in healthier environments as adult obesity surpasses 35% in over 20 states.

By Mizuki Uchiyama

NEW YORK, Dec.14, 2024

An overweight woman in a Park. Credit:FMT | License details

Adult obesity rates have reached alarming levels, with 23 U.S. states reporting rates of 35% or higher, according to recent Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) data. Before 2013, no state reached this threshold, but today, states in the Midwest and South — such as Mississippi, West Virginia, and Arkansas — are among the hardest hit. The trend underscores a growing health crisis exacerbated by limited access to affordable healthy foods and safe physical activity spaces.

"Obesity is not just about individual choices; it reflects systemic challenges like food deserts and inequitable health care," said Dr. Sarah Armstrong, a professor of population health sciences at Duke University. "Community-based efforts, like improving walkable infrastructure and expanding healthy food programs, are critical."

Experts stress that addressing these inequities requires targeted public health strategies and funding for local prevention programs.

In 2023, more than 1 in 5 adults in all U.S. states and territories had obesity.
